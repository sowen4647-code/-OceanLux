Description of the site and who it is for:

As a team we decided on creating a website dedicated to our own fictitious cruise line called OceanLux.
The website is a hub for everything cruise related. It has destinations, showcasing the multitude of beautiful vacation destinations that OceanLux offers to its guests.
It features a fine dining page, a page that we wanted to make sure popped as fine dining was one of the main selling points of our cruise and our website.
As well he has a page showing off all the amazing activities in store for the cruise. Overall the website is a tried and true cruise line website, showcasing all the necessary information for anyone looking to book a cruise.

Our primary user for this cruise line is older couples, families, and singles, looking for a great cruise experience. We wanted to focus on older folks being our primary users as not only does it allow us to keep the website simple and sleek but it gives us opportunity to work more on certain ui and ux that would challenge us but make using the website for anyone no matter how technologically sound they are, an absolute breeze. As well a lot of the destinations, activities and dining experiences are primarily centered around older age ranges, originally the website was just supposed to be a restaurant but we decided to expand it to an entire cruise line, but still keeping a good portion of focus on a great dining experience, something older customers love.

The secondary users for this website are families and younger couples. As with all cruise lines there isn't just one user or customer that they are looking for, and it's the same with oceanlux. We wanted to focus primarily on older users, yes, but also make the website, design, and all the cruise related ideas to also have families and younger couples in mind. It works very well for us as well, we design the website, make it incredibly user friendly for older users and in turn it makes it easy and appealing to use for any number of generations or ages to use.

The tertiary users were probably the biggest no brainer out of all of them, general vacation lovers and cruise goers. This speaks for itself as we are a cruise line and we are selling a vacation and experience, any person interested in an experience like this or a large grand vacation where they can lay back and take it all in would love Oceanlux. Throughout the process we kept the tertiary user in the back of our mind as a guide of sorts.


How to naviguate the site:

Users can use the navigation bar at the top of the page to move between different pages. Home, destination, activities, dining and Book Now. Each page has its own purpose. On the home it presents a nice overview of the cruise, the destinations presents potential places where the cruise will go, the dining page showcases different meals, the activities page, explores different experiences, the boook now page allows users to contacts agents and view frequently asked questions. Overall, the site allows users to quickly find information, explore options, and move smoothly toward booking their trip.
